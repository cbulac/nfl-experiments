## Key Findings

### 1. Tyreek Hill is the Elite Separation Leader

**Tyreek's 7.08 yards/second means:**
- At **2 seconds:** 8.4 yards of separation (quick routes)
- At **3 seconds:** 15.5 yards of separation (intermediate)
- At **4 seconds:** 22.7 yards of separation (deep routes)

**Comparison to Avg NFL Receiver (5.80 yards/sec):**
- Tyreek gains an extra **1.28 yards per second** compared to league average
- Over a 3-second play, that's **3.84 yards more separation**
- This is the difference between contested catch and wide open

### 2. Tight Ends Naturally Have Lower Separation

**TE Rankings:**
- Travis Kelce (KC): 4.31 yards/sec - Rank #31
- Dalton Schultz (HOU): 4.40 yards/sec - Rank #30
- Hunter Henry (NE): 5.01 yards/sec - Rank #29

**Why?** TEs run shorter routes (seam, flat, cross) with less vertical stretch compared to WRs running go/post/corner routes.

### 3. NO Correlation Between Separation and Completion %

**Correlation: r = -0.093** (essentially zero)

**Highest Completion % Receivers:**
1. Brandon Aiyuk (SF): 74.1% - Separation rank #24 (5.38 yds/sec)
2. DeVonta Smith (PHI): 73.2% - Separation rank #8 (6.44 yds/sec)
3. Courtland Sutton (DEN): 73.8% - Separation rank #27 (5.65 yds/sec)

**Why No Correlation?**
- QB accuracy matters more than WR separation
- Scheme fit (WCO vs Air Raid affects completion %)
- Target depth (deep routes = lower completion regardless of WR)
- Defensive quality faced

**Key Insight:** Elite separation ability creates **opportunity**, but doesn't guarantee **conversion**. Tyreek's 67.8% completion is good but not elite, suggesting Tua could optimize timing/accuracy.

## Limitations

### 1. **Position Bias**
- TEs naturally run shorter routes (Kelce, Schultz, Henry in bottom 5)
- Unfair to compare TE to WR separation
- Should separate analyses by position

### 2. **Scheme Dependence**
- West Coast Offense = short routes = lower separation
- Air Raid = deep routes = higher separation
- Not entirely attributable to WR skill

### 3. **QB Quality**
- Bad QBs extend time to throw (scrambling), inflating route distance
- Elite QBs get ball out quick, deflating route distance
- May bias results

### 4. **Sample Size Variance**
- Some receivers have 254 plays (Hunter Henry)
- Others have 470 plays (CeeDee Lamb)
- Larger samples more stable

### 5. **Single Season Data**
- 2023 only
- Injuries, new offenses, age could affect results
- Multi-year analysis would be more robust

## Methodology

**Player Selection:**
- Most targeted receiver from each NFL team (32 total)
- Based on `route_of_targeted_receiver` field in supplementary data

**Metrics Calculated:**
1. **Yards Per Second:** Slope of linear fit (route distance vs time to throw)
   - Measures how quickly WR gains separation as play develops
2. **Route Efficiency:** Average (displacement / total distance)
   - Measures route precision (1.0 = straight line)
3. **Max Speed:** Average of maximum speed reached per play
4. **Completion %:** (Completions / Targets) √ó 100

**Statistical Methods:**
- Linear regression: Route distance ~ Time to throw
- Pearson correlation: Separation vs completion %
- Z-scores for ranking interpretation
